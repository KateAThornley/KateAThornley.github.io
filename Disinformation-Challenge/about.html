THE PROBLEM

In consuming news and content online, users tend to prefer content that aligns with and reaffirms their existing beliefs, this is known as the echo chamber effect (Jamieson & Cappella, 2008; Sunstein, 2002). The echo chamber effect pushes people into silos and exacerbates polarization, particularly with the rise of online news consumption and social media. The tendency to fall into an echo chamber can be fed by personal preference and habits (Dubois & Blank, 2018), or by filter bubbles- which is when news aggregators’ and social media platforms’ algorithms only serve you content which affirms your existing belief (Pariser, 2011). When users are stuck in echo chambers online, they are more susceptible to disinformation because citizens lack a common understanding of shared facts (citation needed). By having their own beliefs reconfirmed and echoed back to them, users also don’t develop and practice the critical thinking skills necessary for identifying and avoiding misinformation (citation needed).

THE APPROACH

At KickBait, our approach to countering disinformation online is to challenge the echo chambers that users find themselves in. We have done this by creating an educational tool which allows users to situate their own news consumption habits within the Canadian media ecosystem, based on the level of political polarization of the content they consume and its credibility. This tool encourages critical thinking and creates an opportunity for users to critically reflect on their online news consumption habits. We believe that if users can recognize that they are in an echo chamber, they will be better equipped to take actions to get out the echo chamber. This tool encourages users to develop positive habits to get out of echo chambers including: Reading content they disagree with; Checking a news source that’s different from what they normally read; and Trying to confirm political information by searching online for another source (Dubois, 2017).

THE METHODOLOGY

We believe transparency in how this tool has been created is essential. It is necessary to share the methodology for ranking within each category, so reasonable people of differing political beliefs can rank the same sources and come up with similar results.
